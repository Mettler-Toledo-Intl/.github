# Mettler-Toledo Open Source Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

At Mettler-Toledo, we are committed to fostering a welcoming, respectful, and inclusive open-source community. We pledge to ensure that all members, contributors, and leaders create an environment free from harassment and discrimination for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual orientation.

We strive to embody Mettler-Toledo’s core values of integrity, respect, responsibility, and inclusion in all interactions within our open-source projects.

## Our Values

**Integrity:** We uphold honesty, transparency, and ethical behavior in all contributions, respecting intellectual property rights and ensuring accountability.

**Respect:** We treat everyone with dignity and value diverse perspectives, fostering a collaborative and supportive environment.

**Responsibility:** We take ownership of our actions and contributions, learn from mistakes, and engage professionally.

**Inclusion:** We embrace diversity and actively create an environment where everyone feels welcome and empowered to contribute.

**Compliance:** We adhere to applicable laws, regulations, and Mettler-Toledo policies to ensure ethical and lawful participation.

**Sustainability:** We consider the social and environmental impact of our contributions, supporting responsible innovation.

## Our Standards

To maintain a positive community environment, we expect all participants to:

* Demonstrate empathy, kindness, and respect toward others
* Value diverse perspectives and experiences
* Provide and receive constructive feedback graciously
* Take responsibility for actions and learn from mistakes
* Prioritize the well-being and success of the overall community

Unacceptable behaviors include, but are not limited to:

* Sexualized language or imagery, and unwanted sexual attention or advances
* Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
* Harassment or intimidation, whether public or private
* Sharing private information without explicit consent
* Any conduct inappropriate in a professional, collaborative environment

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Community leaders and maintainers are responsible for clarifying standards, enforcing this Code of Conduct, and taking fair corrective actions when violations occur. They may remove, edit, or reject contributions that do not align with this Code and will communicate moderation decisions when appropriate.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all Mettler-Toledo open-source community spaces and whenever an individual represents the community publicly, including official communications and events.

## Reporting Violations

Incidents of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior should be reported to the enforcement team at osco@mt.com. All reports will be handled promptly, confidentially, and with respect for the privacy of all involved.

## Enforcement Guidelines

### 1. Correction

**Community Impact:** Use of inappropriate language or unprofessional behavior.

**Consequence:** A private warning and explanation, with a possible request for a public apology.

### 2. Warning

**Community Impact:** A violation through single or repeated incidents.

**Consequence:** A warning with restrictions on interaction for a specified time. Continued violation may lead to further action.

### 3. Temporary Ban

**Community Impact:** Serious or sustained inappropriate behavior.

**Consequence:** Temporary ban from community interactions and communication.

### 4. Permanent Ban

**Community Impact:** Repeated violations or severe harassment or aggression.

**Consequence:** Permanent ban from all community interactions.
